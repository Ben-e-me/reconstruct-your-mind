Every belief your mind holds was learned at some point. 

And what can be learned can also be *updated*.

Not through force.

Not through positive thinking.

But through [curiosity]{organic-beat}.

Whenever you genuinely question something your mind has treated as certain, your brain enters a state where change becomes possible.

As you read, don't try to convince yourself of anything.

Simply notice what happens when certainty gives way to *curiosity*.

# The Permission to Choose Yourself

Before you begin~…~

For the next few minutes, I'd like to invite you to do something your mind rarely gets the chance to do.

::: stack
Not to learn.

Not to solve.

Not even to change.
:::

Just to *wonder*.

There is something remarkable about genuine curiosity.

When we become curious about a belief we've always taken for granted, our mind begins to hold it differently. 

What once felt like an unquestionable truth becomes a possibility. 

And possibilities can be explored.

You don't have to convince yourself of anything while reading these words.

You don't have to agree with them.

Simply notice what happens when certainty gives way to curiosity.

Sometimes that is where *freedom* begins.

As you continue reading, notice the __weight__ of your body where you are.

Notice the sounds around you.

Nothing needs to change.

You are simply here.

And perhaps, somewhere inside you, there is a quiet belief that has been with you for a very long time.

::: stack
A belief that says~…~

"I should be who other people need me to be."
:::

Maybe it has different words.

Maybe you've never said it out loud.

But perhaps you've lived as if it were true.

::: stack
Don't try to answer.

Just notice.
:::

I wonder…

When did you first learn that other people's expectations mattered more than your own?

{left} A face?

{right} A feeling?

You don't need to find the answer.

Just allow your mind to search.

Why does that feel more acceptable?

Who taught you that?
